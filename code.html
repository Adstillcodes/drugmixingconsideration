biotech Mechanism of Interaction

NSAIDs like Naproxen can reduce the antihypertensive effect of ACE inhibitors like Lisinopril. This occurs through the inhibition of renal prostaglandins, which are responsible for maintaining renal blood flow.

"The simultaneous administration may lead to a significant decrease in glomerular filtration rate (GFR), potentially precipitating acute renal failure in elderly or dehydrated patients."

Clinical studies indicate a mean increase in blood pressure of 5-10 mmHg when these classes are combined over a 7-day period.
